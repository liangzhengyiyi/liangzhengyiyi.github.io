Constructing Ideological Space: Estimating Political Attitudes through Chinese People’s Consumption of Books

Abstract Ideology has been a focus issue in the domain of political science. Researchers have long depended on a survey to measure political attitudes, but the method is expensive and limited to time and space. Hence, a great many studies took an unconventional approach to quantify ideology based on discrete behavioral data, including roll-call votes and social media’s endorsement, and generated reliable outcomes. This study contributed to that promising research avenue by estimating ideology through people’s reading patterns.

The Geometry of Culture: How Chinese perceive and apprehend politics

Research Question How do Chinese perceive and apprehend politics? Methodology A public-available word embedding dataset trained by the Word2vec/skip gram algorithm is obtained to examine political connotations. The word embedding model is appropriate to represent political associations based on three reasons. First, it was trained by a large corpus covering various subjects, such as People’s Daily News, Baidu Encyclopedia, Weibo (equivalent of Tweeter) and literature. Specifically, the corpus encompasses political coverage on the news outlets and political discussions on social media, which enables the embedding model to capture underlying political connotations among words.

How to expedite the process of data crawling?

Recently, I crawled a set of users’ book collections from an online community. First I randomly generated about 1 million user ids and obtained book collections by iterating the search. Because some user ids were invalid, over 110,000 users’ book collections were acquired eventually. To crawl a large dataset, web crawlers have to confront a major challenge, which is how to expedite the process since most websites designed Anti-crawlers tactics to obstruct crawling.
